Presented at FLICS 2026 — Valencia, June 9–12 (Session 6, June 9)

“Client-Conditional Federated Learning via Local Training Data Statistics” was presented at the 2nd International Conference on Federated Learning and Intelligent Computing Systems in Valencia. The method conditions a single shared model on a compact statistic that each client computes from its own training data — handling heterogeneous client distributions, whether they differ in input distribution or in labelling convention, with no extra communication or private-information disclosure beyond standard federated averaging, and no per-client cost. Across 97 configurations it matches an oracle that knows the true client clusters, and exceeds it under combined heterogeneity. Published in IEEE proceedings (Scopus / DBLP indexed); the extended version with full experimental appendices is on arXiv.
