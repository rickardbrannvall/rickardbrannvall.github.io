Rickard Brännvall

News & Updates Papers

Senior researcher in machine learning at RISE Research Institutes of Sweden. Background in physics and finance, previously in quantitative analysis and regulatory capital modeling at Citigroup in Tokyo and London. Current research focus:

  • Quantitative methods for AI privacy risk assessment and regulatory compliance
    (GDPR, EU AI Act)
  • Optimization of privacy–utility trade-offs using privacy-enhancing technologies (PETs)
  • Statistical robustness of membership inference attacks against AI systems
  • Co-design of Transformer neural networks efficient under homomorphic encryption;
    a quantum-resilient technology for end-to-end secure data processing
  • Decentralised secure processing environments for health data
  • Scalable heterogeneous federated learning

Recent Projects

  • LeakPro Phase II (Vinnova, 2026–) — Phase II of the LeakPro project, focusing on generative language models, privacy risk audit, model validation for regulatory compliance, and optimisation of privacy-enhancing technologies.
  • LeakPro (Vinnova) — Development of the LeakPro framework for AI privacy risk assessment, supporting Data Protection Impact Assessments and regulatory compliance under the GDPR and the EU AI Act. arXiv
  • SARDIN (Vinnova) — A decentralised sandbox platform for secondary use of health data, built on ColonyOS, in preparation for the European Health Data Space (EHDS) in Sweden. The platform sends analysis code to regional edge nodes rather than centralising personal data. Builds on differential privacy and cryptographic aggregation protocols. DiVA report
  • CryptoBuildingBlocks — Co-design of neural network architectures efficient under fully homomorphic encryption (FHE).
  • HEIDA (Vinnova) — A research project on secure sharing of healthcare data using homomorphic encryption. Developed software tools and demonstrated privacy-preserving analysis and machine learning with health data. DOI
  • VINTER (Vinnova Innovation Competition, 2022) — Technical lead for RISE’s winning team in Vinnova’s innovation competition, on an encryption-based solution for secure computation with health data. DOI
  • NGI Explorer Fellowship (2021) — Research fellowship for a collaboration on homomorphic encryption with Binghamton University, New York.
